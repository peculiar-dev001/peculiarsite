REPRODUCTION AND DEVELOPMENT

Reproduction is defined as the ability of living organisms to give rise to new individuals of the same species.It ensures the existence of that species of organism from generation to generation. It involves the fusion of the male gametes(sperm cell) and the female gamete (ova of female egg cell) to form a zygote which later develops to a young offspring.

TERMS USED IN REPRODUCTION

1. FERTILIZATION

The male ejects the sperms with great force into the cervix and the upper part of the female vagina.The sperms move rapidly through the cervix and uterus to the upper part of the oviduct,it releases its acrosomal enzymes when it bumps into an ovum. These enzymes are lyctic i.e they can dissolve cells and membrane. They help the sperm to move through the follicle cells that surround the ovum and penetrate the zona pellucida of the ovum.The sperm then moves into the ovum leaving its tail behind.As soon as the sperm enters an ovum, the zona pellucida undergoes changes that prevent other sperms from penetrating the ovum. Thus, although millions of sperms are injected during copulation ,only one sperm finally fertilizes an ovum.

2. PREGNANCY/ GESTATION

The zygote divides into two halves. These then continue to divide to form a solid ball of cells. This soon becomes a hollow ball filled with fluid. A knob of cells form at one side within this ball. This gives rise to the embryo. All these development takes place within seven (7) days after fertilization (around 14-21 days of the menstrual cycle). As the embryo grows, the uterus becomes bigger to contain it. All the major organs are formed in the embryo by the end of 10-12 weeks. From this stage onwards, the human embryo is known as FOETUS.As the foetus grow, it becomes active . By the end of 16 weeks, the mother can usually feel the baby move.In humans, the gestation period is 36 weeks

Trimesters of pregnancy

A pregnancy is divided into three trimesters;each about 3 months or 13 weeks long totaling approximately 40 weeks.
a. First trimesters (weeks 1-12)

  • Fetal development: The baby's basic body structure and organ system develops
  • Parental experience: Common symptoms include fatigue,nausea,breast tenderness and frequent urination.
  • Importance: This is a crucial time for fetal development and many miscarriages and birth defects can occur during this period.
b. Second trimester (week 13-26)
  • Parental experience : Early symptoms like nausea may disappear, and you may feel more energy. Some new discomfort may arise such as back pain, constipation or heart burn.
  • Milestone: Ultra sound can determine the baby's gender during this period.
c. Third trimester (weeks 27-40)
  • Fetal development: The fetus grow rapidly and gain weight, positioning itself for birth.
  • Parental experience:Shortness of breath, lower back pain, increase need to urinate, difficulty in sleeping
  • Preparation:The baby drops into your pelvis preparing for delivery
3. PLACENTA

This is a disc-shaped structure composed of material tissue (uterine endometrium) and embryonic tissue. It is connected to the foetus by a cord called the umblical cord that carries umblical artery and vein from the foetus.Trophoblast(a thin barrier the maternal and foetal blood supplies) allows exchange of materials between them(oxygen and nutrients). Certain maternal antibodies also pass into the foetus providing it with immunity against the associated diseases.About 8 weeks after fertilization,the placenta becomes the main producer of progesterone and oestrogen.These hormones maintain the uterine endometrium throughout the rest of the pregnancy.

4. BIRTH

The actual process of birth involves the following stages:
(i) The dilation of the cervix to allow the head to pass through which leads to bleeding called 'show'
(ii) The rupture of the amnion with fluid escaping through the vagina to the exterior also known as breaking of water bag
(iii) The expulsion of the foetus through the dilated cervix and vagina by the forceful contractions of the uterine and abdominal muscles
(iv) The expulsion of placenta after birth

The expulsion of Placenta(after birth)

5. LACTATION:During pregnancy, progesterone and oestrogen cause the milk glands to develop in the mother's breast. The anterior lobe of the pituiary releases ahormone, prolaction, which stimulates milk production after birth.

REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH

1.Contraception:is a method used to prevent pregnancy and manage sexual and reproductive health.Types of contraceptive methods include;
i. Barrier method: Physically blocks sperm from reaching the egg e.g male condoms, female condoms, diaphragms and cervical caps.
ii. Hormonal methods: Use hormones to prevent ovulation,thicken cervical mucus or thin the uterine lining e.g
  • The Patch: worn on the skin and changed weekly.
  • Vaginal ring:inserted into the vaginal and changed monthly
  • Injectables(Depo-provera):injections given every 3 months.
  • Implants (Nexplanon):a small rod inserted under the skin of the upper arm effective for several use.
iii.IUD(INTRA UTERINE DEVICES):Small devices inserted into the uterus e.g
  • Hormonal IUDs:Release progestin effective for 3-8 years
  • Copper IUDs:Non hormonal, create an inflammatory reaction toxic to sperm and eggs effective for up to 10 years
iv.Permanent method(sterilization)e.g
  • Tubal ligation(females):tying the tubes
  • Vasectomy(males):Cutting or sealing the Vas deferens
V.Emergency contraception: used after unprotected sex or contraceptive failure to prevent pregnancy e.g copper IUD and emergency contraceptives pills(ECPs) vi. A.Female infertility

ii.Damage to fallopian tube such as:Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)
iii.Uterine or cervical causes such as:Uterine fibroids,uterine polyps,uterine anomalies,cervical mucus problem.
iv.Endometriosis
B.Male Infertility causes
i.Abnormal sperm production or function such as :Low sperm count(oligospermia),Poor sperm motility,abmormal sperm shape(teratospermia),genetic defect and infections.
ii.Problem with sperm deliveries such as:ejaculation issue, cystic fibrosis, erectile disfunction.

4.Reproductive cancer
A.Male Reproductive Cancer:
i.Prostate cancer:it forms in the postate gland,a small gland located below the bladder that produces seminal fluid.The risk increases significantly after age 50.Certain inherited gene mutations e.g BRCA 1,BRCA2,lynch syndrome can increase risk. There are often no symptoms in early stages but when they appear, they include:difficulty urinating,frequent urination,blood in urine or semen,painful ejaculation,pain in the back,hips or pelvis.
ii.Testicular cancer:this cancer is formed in the testicles(testes),which are located in the scrotum and produce male hormones and sperm. Common symptoms include a painless lump or swelling in either testicle,feeling heaviness in the scrotum,sudden collection of fluidin the scrotum,a dull ache in the abdomen or groin etc.
iii.Penile cancer:a rare cancer that forms in or on the penis.Symptoms include a lump,sore,or growth on the penis that does not heal,bleeding or pain from the penis,foul-smelling discharge under the foreskin etc.
B.Female Reproductive Cancer:
i.Cervical Cancer:This start in the cervix,the lower part of the uterus that connects to the virgina.It is always caused by persistent infection with certain types of HPV(Human Ppillomavirus).It weakens the immune system and it is a common sexually transmitted infection(STI) that can affect both men and women. Symptoms include abnormal virgina bleeding, unusual virgina discharge etc.
ii. Ovarian Cancer:It begins in ovaries, which produce eggs and and female hormones. Symptoms can include bleeching, pelvic or abnominal pain, frequent or urgent urination,difficulty in eating.
iii.Uterine Cancer(Endometrical cancer):This begins in the lining of the uterus.Most common symptoms is abnormal virginal bleeding especially after menopause.
iv. Vaginal Cancer:This forms in the tissue of the vagina. Symptoms include abnormal vaginal bleeding,watery vagina bleeding,painful urination,pelvic pain.
v. Vulva Cancer: It is acancer that forms outer surface of the vulva. symptoms itching, pain, tenderness or ulcer on the vulva.
vi. Breast Cancer:This forms on the cells of the breasts. Symtoms include a lump or mass in the breast or armpit ,skin changes ,nipple discharge.
5.Menopause:Is the permanent cessation of menstruation,resulting from the loss of ovarian follicular activity.The average is around 51 years but it can occur anytime between the ages of 40 and 58. It is caused by natural decline in the function of the ovaries.
Phases of Menopause
1.Premenopause:is the transitional period leading up to menopause, which van last for 4-8 years, leading to irregular periods.A woman can still get pregnant during this time
2.Menopause:The point in time 12 months after a woman's last menstrual period.
3.Postmenopause:The years following menopause.Women remain at high risk for health condition due to lower estrogen levels.
Common Symptoms of Menopause
i.Hot flashes and night sweat
ii.Mood swings
iii.Irregular period
iv.Bone density loss
v.Weight gain
Management and treatment
i.Lifestyle Adjustment:Regular exercise, healthy diet, stress management, quitting smoking.
ii.Vaginal estrogen:Localized estrogen therapy for vaginal dryness.
iii.Non-hormonal medication:Anti depressant,gabapentin and clonidine helps to manage mood swings and hot flashes.
iv.Hormone replacement therapy:involves taking estrogen and sometimes progesterone to replace the hormones the body is no longer producing.
